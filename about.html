About Us

It’s why we connect the world’s largest network of tree planting organizations, people, partners, and ambitions — so we can all plant for a better world. 


We make it possible for
Anyone to do good

We are all the network

We can’t do this work alone, so we never do. Our network is our members supporting planting projects around the world and in their own back yards. Our corporate partners giving back to the planet. Cities and campuses committing to community forestry. And our planting partners with their hands in the dirt. No matter who you are, there’s a place for you in this community that’s shaping the future through trees. 



A powerful united force for forests

Since 2016, Trillion Trees has brought together the power of three of the world’s largest conservation organizations – BirdLife International, Wildlife Conservation Society and WWF. We combine decades of experience and measurable success to test and evolve innovative models for forest conservation. Together we have the power to influence international policy and mobilise our implementation network in over 60 countries.

How support becomes action

Our more than 50 years of experience has allowed us to develop relationships with planting organizations around the world who are doing it right. They know their local communities and regions deeply. The magic happens when we connect their local knowledge with the support, guidance, and resources, so the trees we plant can grow the greatest impact for all of us. 



Transparency

We provide a clear view of how support makes a tangible difference. Impact stories from forests and communities demonstrate the difference trees have on the people, plants, and animals experiencing it first-hand. We provide detailed annual reports and a full impact dashboard to those empowering large-scale projects.

National Tree Benefit Calculator

Understanding This Tool

The Tree Benefit Calculator allows anyone to make a simple estimation of the benefits individual street-side trees provide. This tool is based on i-Tree’s street tree assessment tool called STRATUM. With inputs of location, species and tree size, users will get an understanding of the environmental and economic value trees provide on an annual basis.

The Tree Benefit Calculator is intended to be simple and accessible. As such, this tool should be considered a starting point for understanding trees’ value in the community, rather than a scientific accounting of precise values. For more detailed information on urban and community forest assessments, visit the i-Tree website. The National Tree Benefit Calculator was conceived and developed by Davey Tree Expert Co and Casey Trees. This is an i-Tree powered tool.
